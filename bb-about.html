About us

Bedlam Books, a potted history

Emma Hegarty and Marie Alafaci met while studying in the Associate Diploma of Professional Writing & Editing at Holmesglen TAFE in 1995.

They had both worked in other fields but had decided to do the course to follow their passions – writing and editing. During their time at Holmesglen, they were impressed by the quality of ideas of their fellow students and were often asked to read and critique their peers’ writing. They began to think about how they could help authors to improve their writing and reach a commercial audience.

Fast forward to 1999 and after a number of years of thinking about what form this business would take, Marie and Emma decided that a manuscript assessment business was the ideal way to go. Enter Bedlam Books.

The people behind Bedlam Books

Marie Alafaci

Author, corporate trainer and natural organiser helping organisations communicate with their audiences.

Marie Alafaci has publications in biography, environmental science and speculative fiction; taught Professional Writing & Editing at TAFE; worked for the Australian Society of Authors; reviewed books; written feature articles for magazines and been both a panellist and member of the advisory board for the Emerging Writers’ Festival.

She teaches short courses on grammar and punctuation for RMIT and is a manuscript assessor and tutor for Writers Victoria. She has been a judge on a number of writing competitions and had honourable mentions or second place wins in a number of writing awards herself.

Emma Hegarty

Editing, publishing, research management

Emma Hegarty has worked in the publishing industry as a publisher, production manager, copy-editor and desktop publisher.

She has also been a writer and designer for a local newspaper, and a student media adviser and rights mediator in the TAFE system in Victoria and Queensland.

She has tutored in publishing studies at a tertiary level, and has a broad range of writing credits from journalistic pieces in local newspapers to chapters in two books (one on Australian Rules football and the other on the history of book publishing in Australia).
